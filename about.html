Why UX?

I chose UX because it sits at the perfect intersection of art and practicality. A field where being creative isn't just encouraged, it's the job. I'm drawn to experiences that feel thoughtful, exciting, engaging, and overall human. I'm always thinking about how design can make people feel something.

A lot of what shapes me as a designer comes from my personal interests and experiences. I'm drawn to visual culture in all its forms, animation, illustration, graphic design, and the way storytelling can be communicated through a single image. Growing up immersed in multiple languages and cultures gave me an early sensitivity to how experiencing new and exciting things changes the way we interact with the world, which is something I carry into my design thinking.

Interests

I spend a lot of time walking and outdoors with my dog, and there's something about stepping away from a screen that clears my head and opens me up to new perspectives. I'll often come back to a project with a fresh angle I wouldn't have found otherwise.

I watch a lot of anime, particularly shoujo, and over time I've started noticing how intentional the design choices are; the way color, framing, and other elements are used to shape how a viewer emotions or feelings in a given scene. It's made me much more aware of how visual decisions influence emotion and perception.

I also love shopping at thrift stores. Part of the appeal is the hunt, but also how I start thinking about each piece as a design object; how its shape, color, and style can work within a larger frame, whether that's building an outfit or pulling together a room. This is a very similar thinking for me as design composition, just in a different context.
